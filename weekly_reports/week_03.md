## Key Findings

- AI chatbot service quality can influence customer trust, satisfaction, customer experience, continued usage intention, and loyalty.
- Personalization, response speed, problem-solving ability, perceived usefulness, and service reliability are important AI-powered customer service factors.
- Trust and satisfaction frequently appear as mediating factors between AI service quality and loyalty-related outcomes.
- Privacy risk, lack of empathy, and over-reliance on AI are common limitations or risks in AI-powered customer service.
- Loyalty program studies show that reward design, progress framing, customer engagement, and tier-based benefits can influence loyalty behavior.
- Existing studies often discuss customer satisfaction, e-brand loyalty, or continuance intention, but fewer studies directly examine loyalty tier progression in a smart service ecosystem.
- This creates a potential research gap for the group: connecting AI-powered customer service factors with loyalty tier progression, retention, and long-term engagement.

## Preliminary Research Gap

Although many studies have examined AI chatbots, customer satisfaction, trust, and continued usage intention, limited research has connected AI-powered customer service with loyalty tier progression. Existing studies often focus on general loyalty or e-brand loyalty, while fewer studies investigate how AI customer service interactions can support customer movement across loyalty tiers in a smart service ecosystem.
