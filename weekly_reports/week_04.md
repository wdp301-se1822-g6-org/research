## Common Variables Identified

| Variable Group | Variables |

| Risk factors | Privacy risk, low perceived empathy, unreliable chatbot response, over-reliance on automation |
| Mediating factors | Customer trust, customer satisfaction, customer experience, expectation confirmation |
| Loyalty outcomes | Customer loyalty, customer retention, continued usage intention, long-term engagement, loyalty tier progression |

## Key Findings

- Most AI chatbot studies agree that service quality, personalization, interaction quality, and problem-solving ability can improve customer satisfaction and trust.
- Trust and satisfaction appear frequently as important mediating factors between AI-powered service and loyalty-related outcomes.
- Privacy risk and lack of empathy are repeated limitations in AI-powered customer service.
- Loyalty program studies provide useful concepts such as reward design, progress framing, tier-based benefits, retention, and long-term engagement.
- There is a visible gap between AI chatbot research and loyalty tier progression research because the two streams are often studied separately.
- The group can position the research contribution around connecting AI-powered customer service with customer loyalty and tier progression in a smart service ecosystem.

## Preliminary Research Model

The group drafted the following preliminary relationship among variables:

AI-powered customer service factors, including service quality, personalization, interaction quality, problem-solving ability, and perceived empathy, may influence customer trust and satisfaction. Customer trust and satisfaction may then influence customer loyalty, customer retention, long-term engagement, and loyalty tier progression. Privacy risk may negatively affect customer trust and satisfaction.
