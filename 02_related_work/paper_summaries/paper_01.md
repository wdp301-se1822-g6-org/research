# Paper 01 Summary

## Citation

**Title:** How Do AI-driven Chatbots Impact User Experience? Examining Gratifications, Perceived Privacy Risk, Satisfaction, Loyalty, and Continued Use  
**Authors:** Cheng, Y., & Jiang, H.  
**Year:** 2020  
**Source:** Journal of Broadcasting & Electronic Media  
**DOI/Link:** https://doi.org/10.1080/08838151.2020.1834296

## Problem

The paper investigates how AI-driven chatbots influence user experience, satisfaction, loyalty, perceived privacy risk, and continued use. It addresses the problem that organizations increasingly deploy chatbots in customer-facing services, but the effects of chatbot gratifications and privacy concerns on user loyalty are not fully understood.

## Method

The study uses a quantitative survey-based research design. It examines relationships among chatbot gratifications, perceived privacy risk, satisfaction, loyalty, and continued use through statistical hypothesis testing.

## Dataset

Survey responses from chatbot users. The data captures users’ perceptions of chatbot benefits, privacy risk, satisfaction, loyalty, and intention to continue using chatbots.

## Evaluation

The study evaluates the proposed relationships among constructs using statistical analysis. Key constructs include perceived gratifications, privacy risk, satisfaction, loyalty, and continued use.

## Results

The findings suggest that chatbot benefits such as usefulness, enjoyment, and interaction can improve satisfaction and loyalty. However, perceived privacy risk can negatively affect satisfaction and continued use.

## Limitations

The study focuses mainly on user perceptions and does not directly measure actual behavioral loyalty data such as repeat purchases, reward redemption, or tier upgrades.

## Relevance to our topic

This paper is directly relevant because it connects AI-driven chatbots with satisfaction, loyalty, and continued usage intention in customer-facing contexts.

## Possible improvement

Future work can connect chatbot satisfaction and loyalty intention with actual customer behavior data, such as transaction logs, repeated usage, and loyalty tier progression.
