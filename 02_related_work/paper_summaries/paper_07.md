# Paper 07 Summary

## Citation

**Title:** The paradoxes of generative AI-enabled customer service: A guide for managers  
**Authors:** Ferraro, C., Demsar, V., Sands, S., Restrepo, M., & Campbell, C.  
**Year:** 2024  
**Source:** Business Horizons  
**DOI/Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bushor.2024.04.004

## Problem

The paper discusses the opportunities and contradictions created by generative AI in customer service, including quality, empathy, personalization, privacy, and operational risks.

## Method

The article is a conceptual and managerial paper that synthesizes insights about generative AI-enabled customer service and identifies key paradoxes managers must handle.

## Dataset

The paper does not use a primary empirical dataset. It draws on prior literature, industry examples, and conceptual analysis.

## Evaluation

The paper evaluates generative AI-enabled customer service conceptually by identifying tensions, benefits, risks, and managerial implications.

## Results

Generative AI can improve service scalability, personalization, and efficiency, but it may also create risks such as low perceived empathy, privacy concerns, over-personalization, and reliability issues.

## Limitations

The paper is conceptual and does not test a quantitative model or provide empirical evidence for loyalty outcomes.

## Relevance to our topic

This paper is useful for explaining why AI customer service should be evaluated not only by efficiency but also by trust, empathy, privacy, and customer perception.

## Possible improvement

Future work can empirically test how generative AI customer service paradoxes affect customer satisfaction, retention, and loyalty tier progression.
