# Paper 08 Summary

## Citation

**Title:** The influence of artificial intelligence chatbot problem solving on customers’ continued usage intention in e-commerce platforms: an expectation-confirmation model approach  
**Authors:** Gao, J., Opute, A. P., Jawad, C., & Zhan, M.  
**Year:** 2025  
**Source:** Journal of Business Research  
**DOI/Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jbusres.2025.115423

## Problem

The paper examines how AI chatbot problem-solving ability influences customers’ continued usage intention in e-commerce platforms.

## Method

The study applies the expectation-confirmation model to explain how chatbot problem solving affects confirmation, satisfaction, trust, and continued usage intention.

## Dataset

Survey data from customers who have used AI chatbots on e-commerce platforms.

## Evaluation

The study tests relationships among problem-solving ability, expectation confirmation, satisfaction, trust, and continued usage intention using quantitative analysis.

## Results

AI chatbot problem-solving ability can strengthen expectation confirmation, satisfaction, and trust, which then supports continued usage intention.

## Limitations

The study focuses on e-commerce continued use and may not directly measure loyalty program tiers or offline service ecosystems.

## Relevance to our topic

This paper supports the idea that AI customer service should be evaluated by its ability to solve customer problems, not only by response speed or automation.

## Possible improvement

Future research can examine whether problem-solving chatbots improve loyalty behavior such as repeat service usage, reward redemption, and tier upgrades.
