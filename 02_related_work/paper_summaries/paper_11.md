# Paper 11 Summary

## Citation

**Title:** Consumer reactions to chatbot versus human service: An investigation in the role of outcome valence and perceived empathy  
**Authors:** Markovitch, D. G., Stough, R. A., & Huang, D.  
**Year:** 2024  
**Source:** Journal of Retailing and Consumer Services  
**DOI/Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jretconser.2024.103921

## Problem

The paper compares consumer reactions to chatbot service and human service, focusing on the role of perceived empathy and service outcome valence.

## Method

The study uses experimental or quantitative research to compare customer responses to chatbot versus human service interactions.

## Dataset

Customer response data collected from participants exposed to chatbot and human service scenarios.

## Evaluation

The study evaluates perceived empathy, service outcome valence, and consumer reactions toward chatbot and human service.

## Results

Perceived empathy plays an important role in shaping customer reactions, especially when service outcomes are negative. Chatbots may be evaluated differently from human agents depending on empathy and service outcome.

## Limitations

The study focuses on service reactions and empathy rather than loyalty tier progression or loyalty program behavior.

## Relevance to our topic

This supporting paper is useful because empathy is an important dimension of AI customer service quality.

## Possible improvement

Future research can evaluate whether empathic chatbot responses improve trust, satisfaction, retention, and loyalty outcomes.
